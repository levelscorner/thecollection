## 🧠 AI Research & Papers

**CoT controllability** ([OpenAI](https://openai.com/index/reasoning-models-chain-of-thought-controllability)): Their CoT-Control framework finds that reasoning models resist external control of their internal chain-of-thought. The safety argument is counter-intuitive — an uncontrollable CoT is *good* because it can't be hidden from monitors. Worth tracking as a precedent for interpretability-via-transparency arguments.

**Gemma Scope 2** ([Google DeepMind](https://deepmind.google/blog/gemma-scope-2-helping-the-ai-safety-community-deepen-understanding-of-complex-language-model-behavior/)): Open interpretability tooling now covers the entire Gemma 3 family. Sparse autoencoders for open models are becoming a real research infrastructure, not just an alignment curiosity.

**Inference-time scaling** ([Sebastian Raschka](https://magazine.sebastianraschka.com/p/categories-of-inference-time-scaling)): A useful taxonomy of inference scaling approaches — relevant context for evaluating why GPT-5.4 Thinking and Gemini Deep Think represent different bets on how to spend additional compute at inference time.

**LLM evaluation methods** ([Raschka](https://magazine.sebastianraschka.com/p/llm-evaluation-4-approaches)): Four approaches — multiple-choice benchmarks, verifiers, leaderboards, LLM judges. Practically relevant as [AI Is Acing Math Exams Faster Than Scientists Write Them](https://spectrum.ieee.org/ai-math-benchmarks) — FrontierMath is already saturating, reinforcing that benchmark selection is becoming a strategic moat.

**LLM de-anonymization** ([Ars Technica](https://arstechnica.com/security/2026/03/llms-can-unmask-pseudonymous-users-at-scale-with-surprising-accuracy/)): LLMs can unmask pseudonymous users at scale with surprising accuracy. Combined with the invisible-Unicode supply-chain attack, this week surfaced two underappreciated attack surfaces that grow more critical as AI handles more code and text at scale.

**Vector search cost reduction** ([Towards Data Science](https://towardsdatascience.com/649627-2/)): Pairing Matryoshka embeddings with int8/binary quantization achieves ~80% infrastructure cost reduction with manageable retrieval accuracy tradeoffs. Actionable for anyone running production RAG at scale.
