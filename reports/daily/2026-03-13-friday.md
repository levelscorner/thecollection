## 🧠 AI Research & Papers

**[CoT controllability (OpenAI)](https://openai.com/index/reasoning-models-chain-of-thought-controllability)**: The core finding is that o-series reasoning models resist instruction to modify their chains of thought — they can't be told to hide or alter their reasoning. Practically: this makes it harder to use reasoning models as attack vectors against themselves, because you can't prompt them into deceptive planning. The implication for safety is significant — monitorability through CoT may be more robust than previously assumed.

**[LLM evaluation frameworks (Sebastian Raschka)](https://magazine.sebastianraschka.com/p/llm-evaluation-4-approaches)**: A structured taxonomy of the four main eval approaches (multiple-choice benchmarks, verifiers, leaderboards, LLM-as-judge). Essential reading before making infrastructure decisions — connects directly to the point raised in the [AI search evaluation piece](https://towardsdatascience.com/why-your-ai-search-evaluation-is-probably-wrong-and-how-to-fix-it/) about how flawed eval methodology leads to six-figure infrastructure missteps.

**[Inference-time scaling categories (Raschka)](https://magazine.sebastianraschka.com/p/categories-of-inference-time-scaling)**: A taxonomy of test-time compute approaches, covering GRPO and recent reasoning papers. As models plateau on pre-training scaling, inference-time techniques are becoming the primary performance lever.

**[LLM pseudonymity unmasking (Ars Technica)](https://arstechnica.com/security/2026/03/llms-can-unmask-pseudonymous-users-at-scale-with-surprising-accuracy/)**: LLMs can de-anonymize users at scale by correlating writing style and behavioral signals. This is a significant privacy threat — especially for whistleblowers, security researchers, and anyone who assumed pseudonymity on public platforms.

**[Gemma Scope 2](https://deepmind.google/blog/gemma-scope-2-helping-the-ai-safety-community-deepen-understanding-of-complex-language-model-behavior/)**: Open interpretability tooling now covers the full Gemma 3 family.
